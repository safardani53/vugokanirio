Publish date: 2024-04-22

LITTLE People, Big World star Matt Roloff's younger brother Sam has revealed that he speaks with Jacob more often than his other nephews and niece.

Sam talked exclusively with The U.S. Sun about their creative upbringing before Matt starred in LPBW, including his upcoming art exhibit in the de Young Museum in San Francisco, California; and that he stays in touch with Jacob over Jeremy, Zach, and Molly.

Matt's younger brother, who has made a brief appearance on LPBW, is an artist in Sebastopol, California, a 10-hour drive south of Helvetia, Oregon, where Roloff Farms is.

His digital media piece The Rise of the Yellow Vests Act 23 - The Burning of Notre Dame is currently on display at the deYoung Museum through January 7, 2024.

Sam's second exhibit, Discovery of a Giant Frog Skeleton, is also on display through November 18, 2023.

In an exclusive chat with The U.S. Sun, Sam revealed that he stays in touch with Jacob Roloff, 26, over Matt and his ex-wife Amy's other kids.

Sam told The U.S. Sun: "He's been down here to see my house and whatnot, so I probably have a better relationship with Jacob than of all of them.

"He's actually working with my son Max right now at the farm," the artist explained.

Sam said: "They're doing work at the pumpkin patch."

MOVING ALONG!

Jacob appears to be the Roloff patriarch's favorite as Jeremy, Zach, and Molly all continue to put distance between themselves and the spotlight.

An eyewitness exclusively told The U.S. Sun that Matt announced Jacob and Isabel are moving into his humble one-story home on the farm to lucky pumpkin season visitors during a Q&A session on Roloff Farms.

According to the source, Matt invited his fans to sit for a reading of his book inspired by the family dog, Lucy, called Little Lucy Big Race, published in 2018.

After the reading, Matt opened up the floor to fans to ask any questions about the book or the Roloffs' time on LPBW.

A dedicated fan asked about some construction happening around the farm and Matt was more than happy to share some exciting updates.

Matt said: "I've been living in the one-story home, and when I move into the new house, Izzy and Jacob are going to move in where I was."

The one-story home sits on the $4 million Roloff Farms between the family home featured on TLC and the construction for Matt's new dream house.

FAMILY FEUD

As Jacob has fallen onto his father's good side, Zach and Jeremy have put a bit of distance between themselves and the farm after a family feud.

Jeremy has been busy settling into his new $1.5 million farm with his wife Audrey, 32, as they are both currently working on renovations around the fixer-upper property.

Both Jeremy and Audrey left LPBW in July 2018 and run their own podcast, These Are the Days.

Zach and his wife Tori, 32, now live about an hour away from Roloff Farm in a $1 million home in Washington state.

Last month, The U.S. Sun exclusively revealed Zach and Tori are not expected to return to LPBW next season.

The feud began when Jeremy and Zach both presented offers to Matt to buy the farm, but he refused their offers and instead decided to put the land up for sale.

'TIRED OF IT?'

Sam also shared he thinks it's unfortunate that the family farm is on the market.

When asked about the family home on Roloff Farms for sale, Sam remarked: "It's kind of sad."

He explained: "It's a good family farm and hopefully they keep it.

"[Matt] spent so much time building it out - maybe he's just tired of it, I don't know."
