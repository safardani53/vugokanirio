Uefa announce Spartak Moscow Under-19 captain Leonid Mironov will NOT face punishment for allegedly

Publish date: 2024-03-23

UEFA have announced Spartak Moscow's Under-19 captain will NOT face punishment for allegedly racially abusing Liverpool ace Rhian Brewster.

Leonid Mironov will not face action, despite the England Under-17 star's incredible accusation - after Uefa spent three months investigating.

Uefa revealed they appreciate the allegation was made in good faith but no further action will be taken.

Brewster - who scored eight goals including two hat-tricks as England won the World Cup last October revealed: “I got fouled.

"I was on the floor and I had the ball in my hands. One of their players started saying stuff in Russian to the ref.

"I said: ‘It’s a foul, man, what you playing at?’ I was still sitting down at this stage.

“Then their player leaned over me, right down to my face and said: ‘Suck my d**k, you n****r, you n***o.’

“I jumped to my feet and the ref came running over because obviously he realised something had been said.
