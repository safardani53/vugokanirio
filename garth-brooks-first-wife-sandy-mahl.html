Who is Garth Brooks' first wife, Sandy Mahl?

Publish date: 2024-03-21

GARTH Brooks has found a happily ever after with fellow country music superstar Trisha Yearwood.

He was previously married to his first wife, Sandy Mahl, for fifteen years.

Who is Garth Brooks' first wife, Sandy Mahl?

Sandy Mahl, 58, was born on January 16, 1965, in Tulsa, Oklahoma.

According to Wide Open Country, Mahl first met Garth Brooks, 61, while they were both in a bar near Oklahoma State University.

Brooks and Mahl both attended the midwestern college.

Brooks was a bouncer, and Mahl was a patron when they met, as Brooks broke up a fight involving Mahl.

The outlet states: "One night, while Brooks was working at the bar as a bouncer, he came upon a fight between two women, and one woman had gotten her hand stuck in the wall's wood paneling during the fight.

"The woman with her hand in the wall was Mahl, Brooks' future wife."

The pair tied the knot on May 24, 1986.

Brooks soon began climbing to superstar heights as his music career blossomed.

According to Wide Open Country, Mahl is a "talented songwriter" who co-wrote Brooks' songs I've Got A Good Thing Going and That Summer.

As the publication reported, Mahl co-founded an Oklahoma wildlife rehabilitation center called Wild Heart Ranch where she now works as a wildlife rehabilitator and vice president.

The outlet notes that she and Brooks "continue to co-parent their three daughters."

Do Sandy Mahl and Garth Brooks have any kids together?

Brooks and Mahl have three kids together.

30-year-old Taylor Mayne Pearl was born in 1992, 29-year-old August Anna was born in 1994, and 26-year-old Allie Colleen was born in 1996.

Like her father, Allie Colleen is a country music singer and songwriter.

Why did Garth Brooks and Sandy Mahl divorce?

Brooks and Mahl got divorced in 2001 after 15 years of marriage.

The former couple spoke about their relationship in the 2019 A&E documentary titled Garth Brooks: The Road I'm On.

According to Mahl, Brooks' busy schedule as a country music star contributed to their marriage's demise.

According to Good Housekeeping, Mahl said in the documentary: "People constantly wanting your attention and yanking and pulling on him."

"He'd be gone eight to ten weeks at a time. He'd come home, [and] there would be number-one parties, or shows, or CMAs, or ACMs, American Music Awards, so it was constantly going.

"But we both grew apart really, really quickly."

Mahl later said: "I don't think either of us had stopped to think about how this would change our lives."

Despite the demise of their marriage, Brooks says that he and Mahl still have a friendly relationship.
