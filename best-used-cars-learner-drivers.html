The top 10 best used cars for learner drivers - and theyre all super cheap to insure

Publish date: 2024-09-22

BUYING a second-hand car can be a difficult process for many learner drivers, especially when considering price and safety.

But many young Brits often forget to consider how much it will cost to insure their motor before forking out thousands.

A new breakdown by WhatCar? has revealed the top ten best used cars currently on the market for first-time owners.

Each motor falls under a decent insurance group which means they are cheaper to insure for inexperienced motorists.

All the cars can also be driven away second-hand for under £10,000, with some boasting price tags of just £6,000.

Topping the list is the popular Skoda Fabia, which was recently named the best small motor for the second year running.

The Czech motor is comfortable to drive, fuel efficient and has plenty of safety features, including automatic emergency braking and rear parking sensors.

Learners can drive away in a second-hand model with just 13,000 miles on the clock for under £6600.

The popular Volkswagen Polo is another attractive car for Brits looking for their first car.

While it is slightly pricier than other models, the spacious Polo falls into the lowest insurance group which means drivers can afford to spend a bit more on their initial purchase.

Drivers interested in a smaller cheaper version of the Polo can get themselves a bargain in the Volkswagen Up.

The city car is an excellent choice for learners thanks to its spacious interior, powerful engine and sleek design.

It might not be as popular as other small motors, but the Ford Ka + offers plenty of bang for your buck.

The reliable car is a clear favourite with young drivers thanks to its relatively spacious interior and four-cylinder motor.

Despite its small body, the Hyundai i10 is a pleasure to drive and offers plenty of leg room for passengers.

And new drivers can get behind the wheel of one for just £7,790 with just 3,500 miles on the clock.

Spanish automaker SEAT has also quickly become a favourite with younger Brits, with its Ibiza and Mii earning praise for its infotainment system and large boot.
