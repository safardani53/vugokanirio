Professional Career

Danilo Gallinari commenced his professional playing career in 2005 for Casalpusterlengo. It was a team in Serie B1 (third level in Italy).

However, Gallinari was then acquired by Armani Jeans Milano in 2005. Later, he was sent to Edimes Pavia for the 2005-2006 season.

The team was competing in the Italian League second division championship. But Danilo played only half of the season due to an injury.

He averaged 14.3 points, 3.4 rebounds, 0.8 assists, and 2.0 steals in 7 games. Likewise, he was nominated as the best Italian player in the Italian league’s second-division championship.

Olimpia Milano

In 2006, Gallinari played for Olimpia Milano in the 2006-07 Seria-A season and ULEB Cup. Additionally, Gallinari was named the league’s best player (U-22) in his first season.

Similarly, he also won the 2007 Italian All-Star Game 3-point shootout contest during the same season.

Gallinari played EuroLeague for the first time in the 2007-2008 season. He scored 14.9 points, 4.2 rebounds, 1.5 steals, and 1.7 assists per game.

The giant 6’10” towering player recorded a season-high 27 points against Maccabi Tel Aviv in the final game. For this purpose, he was named the EuroLeague’s Rising Star at the end of the season.
