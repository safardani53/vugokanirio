Is Mayweather vs Tenshin a boxing, MMA or kickboxing fight, how do I watch it on TV and live stream?

Publish date: 2024-09-16

FLOYD MAYWEATHER created shockwaves through the sporting world by announcing he will fight Japanese kickboxer Tenshin Nasukawa on New Year's Eve in Saitama.

But is the fight going to be an MMA, boxing or a kickboxing match? And who are the Rizin Fighting Federation who promoting the card?

What has happened?

At a press conference on Monday, November 5, Mayweather announced he has signed with Japanese promoters Rizin Fighting Federation.

The undefeated boxer, 41, will take on 20-year-old Japanese kickboxer Tenshin Nasukawa on New Year's Eve.

The fight will take place at the Roppongi Hill Club in Saitama and be the headline bout of the Rizin 14 card.

What kind of fight will it be - MMA, boxing or kickboxing?

Mayweather vs Nasukawa will be a boxing match, fought at welterweight.

The fight will last three rounds with Nasukawa not allowed to kick for the duration.

There will also be no judges or points awarded, meaning that there will be no winner should it go the distance.

Regardless of the result, no scores will be recorded.

How can I watch it?

You can watch Mayweather vs Nasukawa online through Fite TV right here.

The card will set you back $24.99 (£19.68).

Mayweather's bout will be blocked on the Fite TV stream in North America and Japan, however.

What time will Mayweather vs Nasukawa be on?

The undercard gets underway at 6am UK time.

Saitama is nine hours ahead of GMT time - meaning the full fight could start at around 11am for UK viewers on the morning of New Year's Eve.

Who are RIZIN?

RIZIN Fighting Federation was formed in September 2015.

Former Pride (MMA organisation) president Nobuyuki Sakakibara created RIZIN.

Top MMA names such as Wanderlei Silva and Mirko 'Cro Cop' Filipovic have had fights for the company in the past.

Who is Tenshin Nasuwaka?

Tenshin Nasukawa is a 20-year-old kickboxer.

He was born in Chiba Prefecture, Japan on August 18, 1998.

The star is the current RISE featherweight world champion and is ranked eighth No8 bantamweight in the world by Combat Press.

What have the fighters said?

Mayweather: "I wanted to do something different.

"I wanted to display my skills outside the US and be in a special fight. I want to give the people what they want - blood, sweat and tears."

Nasukawa: "It was a surprise offer but I accepted without hesitation.

"It's the biggest moment in my life and I want to be the man who changes history.

"I'm not particular about the rules, with or without kicks. Boxing rules I wouldn't mind. My opponent looks taller than he actually is, but I think I could hit him."
