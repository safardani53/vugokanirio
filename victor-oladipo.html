Victor Oladipo: Personal Life & Family

Oladipo of Miami Heat was born on May 4, 1992, in Silver Spring, Maryland. But, he was raised in Upper Marlboro, Maryland.

Most people know him as Victor Oladipo, but his real name is Kehinde Babatunde Victor Oladipo. As of now, he is not married and has no children.

Joan Amanze Oladipo and Christopher Oladipo are his parents, who are Nigerian Immigrants.

His mother, Joan Amanze Oladipo, works as a nurse, whereas his father, Christopher Oladipo, had received his Ph.D. in behavioral science from the University of Maryland, College Park.

He presently is a public health executive for Prince George’s County, Maryland. He is the brother of three sisters named Kristine, Kendra, and Victoria Oladipo.

His sister Kendra became deaf while in second grade. Similarly, Victor and Victoria Oladipo are twin siblings.

Victor Oladipo: Education

Victor is well educated and also has completed his bachelor’s. He completed his school at DeMatha High School in Hyattsville, Maryland, and attended college at Indiana University.

Oladipo acquired a bachelor’s degree in kinesiology with an emphasis on broadcast sports communication.

As he was interested in basketball from his school time, he continued to play basketball after graduating. He followed his passion and ended becoming a great professional basketball player.

Professional Career

After playing with Indian Hoosiers for a long time, he was ready to be developed as a professional basketball player. So, in 2013, Oladipo was officially established as a professional basketball player and is still doing so.

Victor has been playing in the shooting guard or point guard position for a long time. However, he started his professional career in NBA with Orlando Magic.

As a late player for Chris Webber’s team, Victor participated in the BBVA Rising Stars Challenge in February 2014.

Then, after their fourth-year team option, the Magic expanded his rookie scale contract for one more season.

Within the first year with the team, he made it to NBA All-Rookie’s first team. In 2013, the NBA.com Rookie survey selected Victor as the second most athletic Rookie.

Then in 2016, Orlando Magic traded him to Oklahoma City Thunder.

In his first game in the Thunder, Oladipo recorded 10 points in 26 minutes. Before joining the Pacers in 2017, he spent a year there.

His hard work was finally paid off in 2018, as he was included in the NBA All-Star team for the first time.

Until 2018, he played with different teams in his professional career and won many tournaments too. But, currently, he is playing with Miami Heat.

Injury

As we know that being a player is not that easy, and they face many injuries. And Victor is no different. The athlete suffered a facial fracture during a practice session on October 24, 2014.

After taking some rest, he quickly returned to the court with a facial protection mask on 14 November.

On November 17, 2018, he was playing a game against Atlanta Hawks. Suddenly when the game was running, Oladipo had an injury in his right knee and left the game in the first quarter.

Due to his injury, he missed 11 games before coming back in action on December 12.

Unfortunately, On January 23, Oladipo again suffered a ruptured quadriceps tendon in his right knee in the game against Toronto Raptors.

His ruptured quadriceps tendon led him out for the rest of the season. Despite his injury, Victor was selected as an Eastern Conference All-Star reserve.

On May 13, Oladipo had surgery to repair the quadriceps tendon in his right knee. After rest to heal his injury, he returned to the NBA on January 29, 2020.
