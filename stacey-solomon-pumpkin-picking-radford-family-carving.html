Stacey Solomon has the 'best day' pumpkin picking with her sons for Halloween as the Radford family

Publish date: 2024-09-23

STACEY Solomon had "the best day" pumpkin picking with her sons at their favourite farm ahead of Halloween.

The mum-of-three, who lives in Essex, took the day off social media yesterday - but posted about their trip out on her Instagram stories this morning.

The Loose Women presenter, 31, wrote: "Yesterday... we spent the day at the farm searching for the perfect pumpkins - Rex was horrified by the human walking sized pumpkin.

"Lots of you asking where you went. We went to @marsh_farm it's the boys Favourite for Halloween and Christmas because they really go for it.

"Costumes, Little shows, a giant farm shop and of course the bouncing things.

"And now we are having an early morning pumpkin carving session - thank you clocks."

Marsh Farm is an "animal adventure park" in Chelmsford, Essex, who charge £17.99 for entrance to the popular Halloween Festival - with a pumpkin included.

Stacey took all three of her "pickles" - Zachary, 12, Leighton, eight, and Rex, 17 months, her son with Joe Swash - for the spooky day out, and the rain failed to dampen their spirits.

Stacey also gushed: "I had the best day with you pickles... even when they decide they want to jump on a soaking wet bouncy thing in the pouring rain covered in muddy sand".

She later showed off Leighton's finished "universe" pumpkin, with saturn, stars and a spooky bat, raving: "It's so good well done!"

Halloween is set to be a rather tamer event this year - with coronavirus and local lockdowns meaning millions can't meet indoors.

But with the spooky celebration now just a week away, Stacey is far from the only mum getting prepped.

Britain's biggest family, the Radfords, also spent their Saturday preparing - with a group pumpkin carving session.
