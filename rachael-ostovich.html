Rachael Ostovich | Mixed Martial Arts Career

Early career

Rachael initially hopped into MMA in 2010. In the next three years, she gathered an unbreakable amateur record of 6-0. She went on and on and made her professional debut in MMA in Hawaii.

Invicta Fighting Championship

Slowly and gradually, professional doors to MMA started opening for her. Rachael opted for the all-female Invicta Fighting Championship in December 2014.

She was placed against Evva Johnson at Invicta FC 10 and took over the match or fight by split decision.

For the second stint of promotion, Rachael was placed against Andrea Lee on September 12, 2015, at Invicta FC 14. She defeated Lee in the third round by verbal submission to an armbar.

Following that, in her third stint for the promotion, Rachael was placed against Ariel Beck on May 7, 2016, at Invicta FC 17. She went undefeated via the split decision. 

In the fourth bout for promotion, Rachael faced Christin Ferea at Invicta FC 21 on January 14, 2017. However, this time around, she lost the battle via TKQ in the third round. This was also the end of her Invicta Fighting Championship journey. 

The Ultimate Fighter

There was news declared in August 2017 that Rachael would be one of the contenders on The Ultimate Fighter 26, where they were in the making of 125-pound women’s champion.

The process to crown the champion was taking place. 

Rachael was placed against Melinda Fabian in the opening round, where she undoubtedly went on to defeat Melinda by rear-naked choke submission.

She faced Barb Honchak in the quarterfinals and lost the stint by unanimous decision.

Ultimate Fighting Championship

Rachael was featured at The Ultimate Fighter 26 Finale on December 1, 2017, against Karine Devorgyan.

She won the match in the first round by armbar submission. Following that, she faced Montana De La Rosa on July 6, 2018, at the Ultimate Fighter 27 Finale and lost the battle in the third round via rear-naked choke.

Rachael was set up to fight against Paige Vanzant at UFC on ESPN+1. The fight between Rachael and Paige was scheduled for November 27 but was postponed to January 19, 2019. 

That, too, was possible only after her doctor made a clear declaration and permitted her to fight, as she suffered some injuries after being involved in domestic violence with her then-husband.

Rachael lost the match against Paige but what matters is she did not give up. She chose to stand up and fight. And her career went on further despite the personal hurdles she was going through. 

Ostarine Positive And 8 Months Suspension

After the ongoing injuries and competing in the UFC three times, Rachael was again back to the game. She was about to face Polyana Viana in August 2019 but was sidelined for some unknown reasons.

And later, she faced Shana Dobson in February 2020 at UFC Fight Night 168. However, she was out due to a failed drug test.

As per USADA officials, Rachael tested positive for Ostarine and GW1516, undergoing a drug test on January 3, ruling her out of the competition.

And according to the policy, both kinds of stuff are subject to ignorance at all times for UFC athletes. Rachael, however, was able to prove that she took the contaminated stuff resulting in flagging.

As a consequence of this scene, USADA suspended her for eight months and got a four-month credit due to time served under a provisional suspension.

After her suspension period was over, Rachael faced Gina Mazaney on November 28, 2020. She lost the fight in the third round by TKO from brutal body kicks from Gina.

UFC released Rachael on December 7, 2020, following three consecutive defeats.

Bare-Knuckle Boxing

The Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship announced that Rachael signed the contract with the organization on April 27, 2021.

Later it was announced that Rachael and Paige were fighting together in the Bare-Knuckle boxing championship. Here Rachael won the fight via unanimous decision.

Racheal returned to the BKFC and signed a new, multi-fight deal in February 2022.

Rachael Ostovich | Personal Life

Diving into the personal aspects of life, her married life has not been that bliss.

Although Rachael tied the knot with Arnold Berdon, who was in the same profession as her, an MMA fighter, in 2019, they divorced, and Berdon was sentenced to four-year probation.

Brutal Assault

Not to mention, the couple shares a daughter, Ruby Rose Berdon (RuRo), together. On November 18, 2018, Rachael was physically assaulted in her hometown of Honolulu.

Her husband hit her in the face, head, and ribs after a night out with family. She fell to the ground, coughed up blood, and puked many times. 

However, Rachael survived the physical attack making a smart escape through a balcony.

The following day, she filed a case against her husband and was granted a temporary restraining order effective until May 19, 2019. 

During the case hearing, Rachael’s lawyer presented the recorded audio by the bystander that clearly stated it was a brutal act by Berdon.

He was heard shouting at her wife, “You are f***** pretty. You can have anyone in your life.” He even threatened her that he would seriously murder her. And what could a girl do after listening to all this?

Rachael was heard weeping in the background. Both of them were drunk, but the risks rose to that certain level where she had run off a balcony to save her life. 

Judge Trish Morikawa sentenced Berdon to four years of probation after hearing from Rachael, who stated, ” I do have the power to move forward, walking in forgiveness, and I have.”

Robert Rawson, Deputy Honolulu Prosecuting attorney, actually initiated to punish him and send him to jail for five years.

But it was Rachael who was kind and strong enough to ask to reduce the jail time. As Rachael knew that her daughter’s mental health might be affected; not being able to see the father for a long time is a little too much for a kid.

She added, ‘I know how crucial it is to have her father present in her life. I hope he gets the help he needs to become a better father.’

After this, Rachael was in the limelight for her controversy with Greg Hardy.
