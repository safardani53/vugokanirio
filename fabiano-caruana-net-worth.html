Fabiano Caruana is an Italian-American professional chess player known as the youngest grandmaster in the history of both Italy and the United States. 

Further, he became a grandmaster at 14 years, 11 months, and 20 days. As of now, Caruana is ranked as the world No. 2 amongst some of the best in the world.

Likewise, the chess prodigy earned his grandmaster title in 2007 and won his first Italian Chess Championship.

Moreover, he was born in Miami to Italian-American parents, where he quickly began playing chess. Caruana played chess for the US until 2005, after which he transferred to Italy.

Soon he began to bag multiple champions in a few years, including four consecutive Italian Chess Championships, three Dortmund Sparkassen Chess Meeting, and many more.

Similarly, he also won the Sinquefield Cup 2014, where he recorded a historic 3103 performance rating.

Furthermore, the grandmaster improved his Elo-rating to 2844 that helped him become the third-highest-rated player in history behind Magnus Carlsen and Garry Kasparov.

Following his successful career, he transferred back to the United States in 2015. He continued his impressive winning streak as he won the FIDE Grand Prix 2014–15.

Not to mention, he won the 2016 US Chess Championship. Fabiano then went on to represent the US on board one at the 42nd Chess Olympiad.

Moreover, he helped secure gold for the US team and bronze in the individual round. In addition, he won the Candidates Tournament 2018, becoming the second-ever American challenger for the undisputed World Chess Championship.
