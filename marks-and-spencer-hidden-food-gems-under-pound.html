I'm an M&amp;S superfan - the nine hidden food gems you need to try and they all cost less than a quid

Publish date: 2024-06-30

AN M&S superfan has revealed the nine hidden gems that she swears by - so if you are looking to cut costs on your food shop, you’ve come to the right place.

Many of us are feeling the strain of rising costs on our wallets, but luckily for you, a savvy shopper and M&S megafan has shared the nine food buys that she always nabs from the high-street chain.

The bargain hunter took to social media to share her foodie recommendations, leaving many open-mouthed. 

Posting on TikTok under the username @prograd.uk, the savvy shopper said: “M&S hidden gems under £1.” 

The first item that the M&S fan recommended was pink lemonade, which will cost you just 85p.

She also urged shoppers to try the chocolate chip cookies, which are also only 85p a pack.

As well as this, if you want convenience without having to splash too much cash, the foodie recommended M&S packs of microwave rice, which are just 65p.

For store cupboard essentials, the social media user recommended the coconut cream, which costs just £1.

And if you’ve got a sweet tooth, she urged people to try the Scotch pancakes, which will set you back only 85p for a pack of six.

Not only this, but the TikToker also recommended the M&S spaghetti, which is only 95p.

If you’re looking for a weekend bite, she also urged people to try the sweet and salt popcorn, which is much cheaper than many cinemas and is only 90p a bag.

If you fancy a bacon sandwich this weekend, you can also nab a half and half whole loaf of bread from the chain, for just 75p.

Finally, the bargain hunter also swore by the M&S bran flakes, which are only 90p, but she described them as “an icon.” 

Many of the items are from the M&S Remarksable value range, which promise M&S quality for a low price. 

The clip was shared just three days ago, but it has clearly left many open-mouthed, as it has quickly amassed a whopping 185,300 views.

But social media users were left divided at the woman’s clip - while some agreed with the shopper’s recommendations, others weren’t so sure about her hailing them as “hidden gems”. 

One person said: “None of these are hidden treasures, they're basic foods you can get for under a quid elsewhere.”

Another added: “How is spaghetti a hidden gem?”

A third commented: “I don’t think rice is a hidden gem.” 

At the same time, someone else posted: “The pink diet lemonade is so grim.”
