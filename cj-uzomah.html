CJ Uzomah: Career

High School Career

Uzomah went to North Gwinnett High School in Suwanee, Georgia. He started his football career as a quarterback.

In his senior year, Uzomah had 2 touchdowns for 238 yards, ran for 74 yards, and had 168 receiving yards with one touchdown.

As a junior, he passed the ball for 1,750 yards and 15 touchdowns and ran for 800 yards with 10 touchdowns. 

College Career

Uzomah attended Auburn University for college. He had a marketing major in college. 

The 6’6″ tall athlete started playing football in his first year as a tight end. He had 29 passes with 7 touchdowns in his first four seasons. 

Uzomah was with the national team in the 2015 Medal of Honor Bowl. He made 1 reception for 29 yards and defeated the American team 26-14. 

On January 17, 2015, the news about Uzomah’s acceptance to the 2014 Senior Bowl came out. 

On January 24, 2015, Uzomah was a part of a South team and made a pass of 9 yards but eventually lost in the Reese’s Senior Bowl.

Uzomah played in the two all-star games but was not invited to perform at the NFL Combine.

Professional Career

On March 3, 2015, he tried his luck at Auburn’s pro day and with college teammates.

The American football player gave his all in the selection process with hard work and dedication. He performed all the techniques and moves he has learned over the years in that one go in front of the officials of NFL teams. There were representatives from 32 NFL Teams. 

By analyzing his talent and skills, the NFL draft analysts expected him to make the 6th or 7th round draft pick or with a free agent. 

Uzomah was ranked as the 12 best tight ends by NFLDraft Scout.com. 

Moreover, the Cincinnati Bengals picked Uzomah in the fifth round (157th overall) of the 2015 NFL Draft. He was one of the seven tight ends in 2015 and was the second tight end drafted by the Bengals. 

Contract with the Bengals, 2015

On May 15, 2015, the Cincinnati Bengals signed a four-year contract of $2.49 million, including a bonus of $212,762 with Uzomah.  

Uzomah went through extensive training at the beginning with his teammates. He had to complete for in the tight end with others. After Termaine Gresham departed from the team, Uzomah got to play as a second tight end. 

Uzomah debuted as a professional for the regular season on December 6, 2015, after playing almost 11 games. He replaced Tyler Eifert as a second tight end.
