Buying perm rods when you have no previous curling experience can leave you with a less than satisfactory product. To help you out, we've compiled the perfect buying guide to ensure you don’t pick the wrong products.

What Are Perm Rods Used For?

Perm Rods are plastic, horizontal tools used to curl hair. They are used to set hair during chemical perm treatments but are now a popular tool to curl natural hair. Some hairstyles that can be achieved using perm rods are as follows:

  • Casual waves
  • Beach waves
  • Partial perm
  • Pin-curl perm
  • Spiral perm
  • Root perm
  • Stack perm

How to Use Perm Rods

Compared to other hair styling tools, perm rods are relatively easy to use. You just have to make sure you use styling products that lock in the curls for a long time. The easiest way to do a natural perm is:

  • Wash your hair thoroughly.
  • Dry your hair out to dampen it, and apply a leave-in conditioner.
  • Brush out your hair prior to wrapping it around the perm rods.
  • Lock the perm rods in place and leave them overnight. In the morning, separate the curls to achieve a full and voluminous look.

    • Types of Perm Rods

    Depending on the length of your hair, you may benefit from one size of perm rod over the other. Some of the most common ones that you can expect to find are:

    Small rods

    Small rods are perfect for people with short hair that is often four inches long. The hair easily wraps around the rods and produces tight curls. Small rods are usually found in 1/8, 1/4, and ⅜ inches.

    Large rods

    Large rods make looser curls that are tightly coiled. They are perfect for hair that is around five or six inches in length. These rods come in 1/2, 9/16, and 11/16 inch buying options. 

    Jumbo rods

    Jumbo rods are great for long hair and make loose waves perfect for beach or summer styles. These rods have a large area to wrap the hair around to cover long hair effortlessly. Since they are so big, you should not expect controlled curls that stay in place.

    Spiral rods

    Spiral perm rods are essential for anyone looking to make classic corkscrew curls. They come in various sizes for people of any hair length. These girls are very different from the traditional styles made from perm rods.

    Natural Hair v. Chemical Perm

    For the longest time, chemical perms have been the top choice of women looking to have curly hair. On the downside, chemical perms are known to permanently alter the structure of your hair and cause significant damage over time due to harsh chemicals like glyceryl monothioglycolate and ammonium monothioglycolate.

    Using perm rods on natural hair can help keep the curls in for up to five days if you do it right. It saves you from hair damage and even helps maintain your hair’s moisture. Most people prefer to use perm rods on natural hair for effortless curls.
