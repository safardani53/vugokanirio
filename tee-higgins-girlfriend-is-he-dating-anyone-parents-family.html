Many have been commenting on social media that Tee Higgins is the father. What is the truth?

Tamaurice William Higgins, or Tee Higgins is American football wide receiver for the Cincinnati Bengals of the NFL.

He was drafted by the Bengals in the second round of the 2020 NFL Draft as the first overall pick.

Higgins made his NFL debut against the Los Angeles Chargers in Week 1 of the 2020 season.

The 24-year-old attended Clemson University, where he played football. There, he won the 2019 College Football Playoff National Championship as a sophomore.

Additionally, the wide receiver was also successful in earning first-team All-ACC in the year 2019. Also, in 2018, the player won the second-team All-ACC.

Tee Higgins Parents

Tee Higgins was born on January 18, 1999, in Oak Ridge, Tennessee, to mother Camilla “Lady” Stewart and father Eric Higgins.

Unfortunately, Eric died on August 30, 2022, at the age of 52. Higgins grew up with his sister, Shakia “Keke” Higgins.

Eric Higgins and Lady Stewart ended their marriage when Tamaurice was young. Before their marriage, the pair were together for more than four years.

After the divorce, Tee started to live with his mother, Camilla. Being a single mother, Stewart gave all the love and support she could to both of their children.

Since childhood, the wide receiver has been in the custody of Stewart, so he is much closer to his mother than to his father.

Lady Stewart Shot In The Head

Camilla Stewart, after parting ways with Eric, was in a relationship with a drug dealer boyfriend. Lady Stewart was also addicted to drugs during that time.

Furthermore, the mother was shot in the head by her own boyfriend and a drug dealer when Tee was just 6 years old.

Tee recalled the time when his teacher pulled him out of kindergarten just after lunch. Then, the young Tee noticed his then-17-year-old sister KeKe in tears when she came to pick him up from school.

Higgins returned home to a crime scene in Oak Ridge, Tennessee. Stewart had already been rushed to the hospital for emergency surgery.

“When we pulled up to the house, it seemed like there were 100 police officers out front, but I remember the yellow tape more than anything,” Higgins said.

Miraculously, Lady Stewart’s treatment was successful, and she survived.
